Lane Thompson

  I am a graduate in computer science at Oregon State University. My focus has been in simulation with an interest in AR. I switched to a CS major from mechanical engineering in 2018 after 3 years in college. While I had a lot of fun studying as an ME, none of the work seemed entertaining enough to start a career in, so I switched.

  My life interests are soccer, gaming, and music, typically in that order. I spent 13 years of my child hood playing soccer and then spent my first two years of college playing on the men's soccer team of Southwestern Oregon Community College. I still play with the Oregon State club team and I am always looking for ways to keep playing and to keep getting better.

  As for music, I am part of a local Blues/Funk fusion band called Church Ladies. We are currently a 5-piece in which I play baritone saxophone. We often play local bars and sometimes larger shows like local festivals. COVID has made it hard to do live shows, but we still get to practice together and even got to record an EP.

  Finally, my last hobby is videogames. Videogames have and always will be a great love of mine, and what made me decide to pick CS when I wanted a change in major. Videogames both as recreation and as an art form are amazing, and I am excited to see how videogames evolve in the coming years.

During my time since finishing my degree I have been learning applications of my graphics knowledge. I have mostly been working using the Unity engine to create small games and simulations. I also am joining a small indie team of developers to work on personal game ideas. On this team I will be the lead on the use of shaders in the game.

  I am excited to have a chance to use the knowledge I gained at University after so many years of studying at work that is exciting and inspiring. I am currently looking for jobs in a couple of fields and I am hoping to gather experience from the professional world so I can narrow down where I want my career to go.
